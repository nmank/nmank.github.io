Bohemian Rhapsody

I didn't finish any of the pitches I started to open. My fear of the unknown paralyzed me. Wasting incredible amounts of energy. I would open 10-20 meters, then lower from a mini anchor, exhausted. When I'm climbing I often feel two forces, something pulling me up the wall, a siren from the summit, pulling me up, move by move. Other times, I feel a weight on my body, my insecurities, uncertainties, fear, pulling me down. A rope yanking me down. A voice inside my head telling myself that I'm not enough, never will be enough, that the next part is too hard, too dangerous, too uncertain. Those days, opening in Logroño, the downwards force almost always beat me. Forcing retreat early, sometimes on easy terrain with decent protection. So it goes, it takes a lifetime to hone the skill of first ascents, with minimal bolts, on limestone. Javi, is much further along this path, with years of experience and a motivation second to none.

Day 1–2

The process of Bohemian Rhapsody began in April 2024. I was two months out of the most intense relationship of my life. Every time I even thought of my ex, I panicked and felt the most intense grief I've ever experienced. To combat this, I was consistently abusing nicotine. The first days on the route, I brought a healthy amount of snuss enabling me to have at least one at each belay. I guess, in a way, this is one of the few climbing experiences that helped me fall out of love with her and more deeply in love with my climbing partners and the times we share together.

The first trip to the route involved 2 days opening the first 4 pitches. The details of this experience can be found in my New routing in Logroño post.

Day 3

Javi went back with another partner to open the final pitch 5. He rappelled in from the top, then attempted to climb from the last belay ledge to the summit. He had to drill one bolt, then tried to push straight up but found insecure, dirty terrain in the 5.11+ to 5.12 grades with ground fall potential. He found a way around, at about 11a-ish with a loop traverse around and to the left. All that was left was to send each pitch of the route.

Javi gave his friend, Tron, a belay to attempt to send as much as possible. Tron made an impressive onsight of the first pitch. The route remained unsent until Javi and I went back in Fall 2025.

Day 4

On our first day back on the route, we took all day to hangdog the route, then descended by rappel in the fading light. I hung my way up the first pitch. Absolutely amazed that someone had onsighted it. Then Javi cruised the second pitch. It was my lead for the 3rd pitch. I made my way up to the runout. Then froze — I was unable to commit to the insecure 11a moves. I kept climbing up, then downclimbing, hanging and repeating. After 30 minutes of uncertainty, I finally committed to the runout and made it to the .75 and black totem placement, then clipped a bolt. After that, it's 5.12 climbing between threads with the odd piece of gear. I hung my way up to the belay, sorting the moves, quieting the voice inside that told me that I wasn't good enough.

Then he took the lead for the traversing 4th pitch. I followed the pitch cleanly. Javi took the last pitch at sunset. I belayed while taking it all in — the beautiful landscape, clouds, and birds in the fading light. This time, I left the nicotine at home. It wasn't needed anymore.

Day 5

We took a rest day. The next day we returned. Javi lead the first pitch, he ended up hanging due to some intense flash pump. I followed without falling. Then I took pitch two. On the crux pitch three, it was Javi's turn to go first. The way he calmly dispatched the runout was more than inspiring. He sent past the only bolt on the pitch, pitched off just after the first set of threads, then hung his way to the top.

It was my turn. This time I didn't second guess the runout. I made it to the bolt first try, then entered sport climber warrior mode — climbing boulder problem after boulder problem, resting as best I could in between. I made it to the anchor without falling. Javi offered me the lead of the 4th pitch. With some uncertainty, and a lot of gear beta from Javi, I managed to free it. Wobbling up the moderate runout finish, out of sight of the belay. Then, the final pitch. Javi even gave me this lead since I hadn't fallen yet.

It was raining on and off while I climbed. But the hard climbing on pitch 4 is steep enough that it stayed dry. When Javi and I reached the summit, we smiled, satisfied with a free ascent. Due to high winds, we chose to descend by bushwhacking and scrambling. While walking down, we chose the name Bohemian Rhapsody for our new route.

I seriously doubt anyone will go repeat our route within the next few years. It's a good route, but it's not great. However, the experience I had with Javi on Bohemian Rhapsody is unforgettable. We've climbed mega classic routes around the world together — Moonlight Buttress, Tague Yer Time, Monkeyfinger, Hearts and Arrows in the USA, and routes in Spain like Melon's Obsession and La Divina Comedia. Although each of these routes was memorable, opening Bohemian Rhapsody surpasses all of them. This was all Javi's idea. He gifted me the experience of a lifetime.

Pitch-by-Pitch

Pitch 1: Boulder past a bolt, clip a thread and traverse right on side pulls, go up past a thread and make a tricky traverse back left at the level of another thread. Go straight up to a pin, then boulder diagonally up and right, then back left to a belay.
Pitch 2: Climb straight up past the belay, dispatching a short boulder problem while slightly trending up and diagonally to the right to a thread. Once at a ledge, traverse left to a belay.
Pitch 3: Climb up off the belay to another ledge, again traverse left, careful for loose rock above the ledge. Clip a thread and back it up with a #1, medium stopper, and/or #.4. Then, head straight up through a runout 6c boulder problem to a bolt. Boulder past the bolt and a number of threads to an alcove. Traverse back right to the crack, follow it past a bulge.
Pitch 4: Traverse right past a thread, navigate up to a second thread. Gear is often hidden and to the right. Once hands are in a hole, place some gear in the back and make a boulder traversing to the right. Climb up past a thread into a dihedral. Mantle out of sight of the belay to a sloping ledge and continue in runout terrain past a thread to a belay.
Pitch 5: Climb straight up past a thread, bolt, and second thread looking for a traverse left below the third thread. Then go up, traverse back right to a crack. Climb the overhanging crack to the summit.
